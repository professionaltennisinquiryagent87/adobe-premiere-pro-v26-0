## 🛠️ Getting started with your project

When you open the application, the welcome screen appears. Choose New Project to begin. Give your project a name and select the folder where you want to store your files.

Import your video clips by choosing File, then Import. Drag your clips into the timeline area to start cutting your video. Use the Razor tool to trim unwanted footage. The AI mask tool allows you to trace objects in your frame for color grading or background changes.

## 🖥️ Using the interface

The workspace contains several panels:

* Project Panel: This area holds your video and audio source files.
* Timeline: This area represents your sequence and shows the order of your clips.
* Program Monitor: This viewer plays your edited video frame by frame.
* Effect Controls: This tab lets you adjust settings like scale, position, and opacity for your clips.

## 🎨 Color correction tips

The Lumetri Color panel offers basic tools to fix your video. Use the Basic Correction tab to adjust white balance and exposure. Select the Color Wheels and Match tab to create a specific mood for your movie. Always check your video against a reference image to verify accuracy.

## 🤖 Leveraging AI masking

Artificial intelligence simplifies complex tasks. Select your clip in the timeline and open the Effects panel. Choose the Object Mask effect. Use the pen tool to draw an outline around the person or object you want to isolate. Premiere Pro tracks the movement of that object across your video automatically, saving you time spent on manual frame adjustments.

## 💾 Saving and exporting your work

Frequent saves protect your progress. Press Ctrl + S on your keyboard to save your project file often. When you finish your edits, select File, then Export, then Media. Choose a format like H.264 for high-quality video that works on most platforms and devices. Ensure your resolution settings match your source footage for the best results.

## 📢 Troubleshooting common issues

If the application runs slowly, close other open programs to free up memory. Check that your display card drivers stay updated, as this improves playback performance. If your footage appears choppy during editing, use the Proxy workflow. This creates smaller, temporary files for editing while keeping your original high-quality files for the final export.
